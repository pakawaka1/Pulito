About Pulito

Pulito was developed by current and past members of the Bitwise Research and Development Cohort. As part of working in the Cohort, we learned the importance of teamwork in a software-development environment. Using our team-building skills, we decided to take this opportunity to challenge ourselves and enter into this yearâ€™s 59 Days of Code Competition on the Smart Cities track.

While considering what kind of smart-city app to build, we noticed that Fresno, along with many other cities, lacked mobile-friendly resources to help provide its citizens with local up-to-date information regarding recycling and waste disposal. Our mobile app, Pulito, seeks to solve this problem by giving users a great easy-to use tool to help find, for example, recycling centers that a scrap aluminum. Additionally, when a user creates a profile, he or she has access to a visualization of their impact on our environment through a variety of data reports.

It is our ethical responsibility to ensure that our environment remains clean for years to come, and Pulito is one resource that can help us achieve this goal.
